Exercício 9.1

Show that the covariance matrix \[\rho = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & .63 & .45\\ .63 & 1 & .35\\ .45 & .35 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \]

for the \(\rho=3\) standardized random variable \(Z_{1}\), \(Z_{2}\) e \(Z_{3}\) can be generated by the \(m=1\) factor model

\[Z_{1} = .9F_{1} + \epsilon_{1}\] \[Z_{2} = .7F_{1} + \epsilon_{2}\] \[Z_{3} = .5F_{1} + \epsilon_{3}\]

where \(Var(F_{1})=1\), \(Cov(\epsilon,F_{1})=0\), and

\[Cov(\epsilon) = \begin{bmatrix} .19 & 0 & 0\\ 0 & .51 & 0\\ 0 & 0 & .75 \end{bmatrix} \]

That is, write \(\rho\) in the form \(\rho = LL' + Cov(\epsilon)\)
